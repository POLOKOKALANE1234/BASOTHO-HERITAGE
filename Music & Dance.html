The brief discription of Basotho music and dances

Dance is a special form of art that may be usedfor expressing emotions, getting rid of worries and aggression, telling a story, sharing feelings, and even praying. Art is a distinctive map of the success of all humankind. It show how people develop in different areas and what they believed in.

DANCES

Litolobonya

A traditional dance performed by women and girls, often at celebrations and village gatherings.

Mokhibo

A traditional Basotho dance performed by women and girls, often at celebrations and village gatherings.

Mohobelo

An energetic and traditional dance exclusively pereformed by Basotho men.

Mokopu

A traditional Basotho folk song and dance performed by young women, often during social gatherings and festivals.

Selia-lia

A tradional dance performed by boys and girls.

Ndlamo

A traditional and powerful dance performed by boys and men.

Setepe

A traditional dance, popularly performed at weddings and other celebrations

Basotho music instruments

Moropa

A traditional Basotho clay drum played primarily by women during grls' initiation rites.

Mamokhorong

This one-stringed violin-like instrument is made from a tin can resoator and played with a friction bow.

Sekhankula

A bowed, sinle-string musical instrument of the Basotho.

Lekolulo

A kind flute played by herd boys.

Thomo

A bow instrument, traditionally played by women, that features a gourd resonator.

Setinkane

A traditional thumb piano , or lamellaphone.

Lesiba

A type of wind instrument with a mournful drone. It is played by her boys by blowind and sucking air through a quill.

Setlo-tolo

A mouth bow, played by men, that produces sound with the mouth acting as a resonator.

Sekebeku sa ts'epe

A traditional Basotho musical instrument, specifically a type of jew's harp, made from metal.

Khabulu-bulu

A wind musical instrument, similar toto a flute or horn, and is often used by herd boys for personal amusement

Merithloane

Ankle rattles worn by dancers to add percussion.

Lekope

A stick pipe instrument made from a river reed with horsehair.

Sekupu

A traditional drum.

Korostina

The Basotho for the concertina, adopted by miners working in South Africa.

Koriana

The Basotho name for the accordition, another instrument that became a staple of famo music.

Lekhitlane

Spiritual horns used by traditional doctors and resonant sound.
